Putting It Into Practice

Adopting these powerful AI tools isn't just a technical exercise; it's a strategic one. Before leveraging AI, organizations must often modernize their applications and infrastructure. The following activities are designed to help you reflect on these crucial first steps.

Activity 1: Modernization Strategies

Identify key modernization strategies in the projects that you are actually working on.

Explanation:

To effectively use cloud AI services like BigQuery ML or Vertex AI, your data and applications must be accessible and optimized for the cloud. This activity prompts you to think about your current projects and identify how they could be modernized. Consider strategies like **re-hosting** (lifting and shifting), **re-platforming** (making cloud optimizations without changing core architecture), or **re-factoring** (re-architecting for a cloud-native environment). A clear modernization strategy is the bridge between your current systems and your future AI capabilities.

Activity 2: Migration, Security & Tradition

In which cases is it not advisable to migrate? How can you ensure security and tradition?

Explanation:

Cloud migration isn't always the answer. This activity encourages critical thinking about when *not* to migrate. Certain systems might remain on-premises due to extreme low-latency requirements, complex dependencies, or strict regulatory/data sovereignty laws. For systems you do migrate, "tradition" (your established business rules and security posture) must be preserved. This means implementing robust cloud security using a shared responsibility model, managing access with tools like IAM, and ensuring data is encrypted both in transit and at rest, thus upholding your security standards in a new environment.
